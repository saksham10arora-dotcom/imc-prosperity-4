# 05 - Bot Forensics: Reverse-Engineering the Simulator

## The Question

Scripts: `18_bot_behavior.py`, `19_bot_coordination.py`, `20_basket_implications.py`

Counterparty names were redacted in R5 (buyer/seller fields blank). But the trade tape itself - timestamps, prices, quantities - still contained information. We asked: can we reverse-engineer the simulator's bot structure from the tape alone?

## The Discovery: 3 Basket Bots

By analyzing which products traded at exactly the same timestamps, we discovered that **100% of R5 trades came from exactly three coordinated basket bots:**

| Basket | Products Covered | Trades per product (D3) | Total trades |
|---|---|---|---|
| **BIG** | 40 products (8 categories × 5) | 733 | 29,320 |
| **PEBBLES** | PEBBLES_XS/S/M/L/XL | 644 | 3,220 |
| **MICROCHIP** | MICROCHIP_CIRCLE/OVAL/SQUARE/RECTANGLE/TRIANGLE | 569 | 2,845 |
| **Total** | | | **35,385 = 100% of tape** |

**Verification:** `733×40 + 644×5 + 569×5 = 35,385 ✓`

Trade-tick sets were *byte-identical* across all members of a basket - when the BIG basket fired, all 40 products had trades at that exact tick, every time, with zero exceptions.

## Behavioral Analysis

### Q1: Is the aggressor informed?

At every basket-tick, the aggressor side was **100% unanimous** across the basket. When the BIG basket fired, it was all-buy or all-sell across all 40 products simultaneously.

But is the direction predictive?

- Pearson correlation (side, next-tick mid move) = **0.015**
- All-buy ticks → +0.15 mean dmid. All-sell ticks → +0.10 mean dmid.
- Both essentially zero.

**The basket bot is uninformed.** Knowing its direction tells you nothing about future price.

### Q2: Does the bot move the market?

Mid-price moves on basket ticks vs quiet ticks:

| Tick type | Mean |dmid| |
|---|---|
| Basket tick | 7.85 |
| Quiet tick | 7.86 |

**Identical.** The bot's flow does not visibly move the mid.

### Q3: Can we predict the schedule?

- Day-to-day schedule overlap: Jaccard index ≈ **0.001** (essentially zero)
- Within-day distribution: **uniform** (147 BIG ticks per quintile, no clustering)
- Inter-arrival: BIG basket fires every ~30 ticks (median), max gap 273

**Cannot front-run.** Schedule is randomized each day with no exploitable pattern.

### Q4: Are aggressor skews exploitable?

| Basket | Buy events | Sell events | Skew |
|---|---|---|---|
| BIG | 358 | 375 | −2.3% (flips sign across days) |
| PEBBLES | 321 | 323 | −0.3% |
| MICROCHIP | 300 | 269 | +5.4% (but D4 reverses) |

**No persistent skew.** Cannot trade a directional bias.

## Implications

These findings reshaped how we thought about the strategy:

1. **Don't chase the tape.** Trade-tape-based signals (aggressor following, flow imbalance from trades) are useless in R5 - there is no informed counterparty.

2. **Our edge is in quote evolution.** 94% of our z-score pair signals fire on quiet ticks (no trade). The spread signal comes from the order book updating, not from trade flow.

3. **The basket bot provides liquidity, not information.** When we want to execute (enter or exit a pair position), the basket bot is our counterparty. It will fill us at random intervals (~every 30 ticks) regardless of our position.

4. **OBI is noise.** Order-book imbalance reflects the same bot's resting quotes, not separate informed flow. OBI signals are generated by the same entity we're trying to trade against.

5. **Fill realism.** With ~733 BIG-basket ticks per day, a passive order at the touch gets filled only when a basket event fires at a price reaching our quote. This is why backtester PnL (mid-fills) overstates real performance.
